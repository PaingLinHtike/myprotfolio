Dota 2
Dota 2 is a 2013 multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) video game by Valve. The game is a sequel to Defense of the Ancients (DotA), a community-created mod for Blizzard Entertainment's Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos. Dota 2 is played in matches between two teams of five players, with each team occupying and defending their own separate base on the map. Each of the ten players independently controls a powerful character known as a "hero" that all have unique abilities and differing styles of play. During a match players collect experience points and items for their heroes to defeat the opposing team's heroes in player versus player combat. A team wins by being the first to destroy the other team's "Ancient", a large structure located within their base.

Game Play
In "Dota 2," two teams of five players each compete to destroy the enemy team's Ancient, a heavily guarded structure located within their base, while defending their own. Players control unique hero characters with distinct abilities and roles, such as carry, support, initiator, and more. The game is characterized by strategic team-based gameplay, where players must coordinate their actions, manage resources, and make tactical decisions to outmaneuver and outplay their opponents. Matches typically last between 30 to 60 minutes and are played on a dynamic map with three lanes, jungle areas, and various neutral objectives. "Dota 2" offers deep gameplay depth, a wide variety of heroes and strategies, and a vibrant competitive scene with tournaments and leagues attracting millions of players worldwide.
